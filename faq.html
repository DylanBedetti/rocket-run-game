What is Rocket Run?
In Rocket Run, players watch as a rocket ascends into the sky, with the multiplier increasing the higher it goes. The goal is simple: cash out before the rocket crashes. The catch? The timing of the crash is unpredictable, making each round a thrilling experience.
Is it fair?
Every outcome of Rocket Run is randomly generated. Low multipliers are likely, larger multipliers are less likely.

You have a 50% chance of the multiplier being between 1 and 2. You also have a 50% chance that the multiplier is above 2.

What sets Rocket Run apart from other similar casino games is the absence of house odds. This means the game does not favor the "house" in any way, offering a fair and transparent gaming experience for everyone. Each player has an equal opportunity to win, making every game round a genuine test of nerve and timing.
Does cashing out early effect the outcome?
No, the outcome is randomly determined before each round, and no action you take will impact what the multiplier will rise to.

Every round in Rocket Run is governed by a Random Number Generator (RNG). This ensures that the outcome of each game is entirely random and fair. The RNG system is designed to guarantee that neither the players nor the game developers can predict when the rocket will crash.
